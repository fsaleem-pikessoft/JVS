The Benefits of Hiring a Tax Professional vs. DIY Tax Software

December 20, 2024 7 min read JVS Tax Services

As tax season approaches, many individuals and business owners face the dilemma of whether to hire a tax professional or rely on DIY tax software. While DIY tools can be convenient and affordable for simple returns, there are significant benefits to working with an experienced professional, especially as your financial life becomes more complex.

When DIY Software Can Work

  • Simple W-2 income with standard deduction
  • No dependents, investments, or special credits
  • Single-state filing, no business or rental activity
  • You are comfortable entering data and following software prompts

Advantages of Hiring a Tax Professional

  • Maximizing deductions and credits: Professionals identify opportunities software may miss, especially for families, homeowners, and investors.
  • Complex situations: Investments, crypto, multiple states, rental properties, K‑1s, self-employment, and itemized deductions require expert handling.
  • Audit support and representation: If issues arise, a pro can communicate with tax authorities on your behalf.
  • Strategic planning: Guidance on entity selection, quarterly estimates, retirement planning, and year‑round tax strategies.
  • Time savings and peace of mind: File accurately and confidently without second‑guessing.

Which Option Is Right For You?

If your tax return includes itemized deductions, self‑employment income, multiple W‑2s, stock/crypto sales, rental properties, dependents, education credits, or multi‑state filing, hiring a professional is usually the better choice.
